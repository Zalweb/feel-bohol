Top Attractions

Bohol's Finest

From 1,200 chocolate-brown hills to sea turtles at a coral marine sanctuary — these are the experiences Bohol is known for.

Landmarks

Major Attractions

Nature

Chocolate Hills

Over 1,200 perfectly cone-shaped limestone hills spread across the municipalities of Carmen, Batuan, and Sagbayan. They turn a striking chocolate-brown during the dry season (January–May) — a geological wonder found nowhere else on Earth.

₱100 – ₱150 entrance
Wildlife

Philippine Tarsier Sanctuary

Located in Corella, this ethical sanctuary is home to the tarsier — one of the world's smallest primates. Visitors must observe silence and maintain a 2-metre distance. Flash photography is strictly prohibited.

₱150 – ₱170 entrance
Experience

Loboc River Cruise

Drift down the emerald Loboc River aboard a floating restaurant while enjoying a Filipino buffet and live cultural performances. The ~1.5-hour cruise winds through lush jungle canopies and is one of Bohol's most iconic experiences.

₱850 – ₱1,000 (buffet incl.)
Nature

Bilar Manmade Forest

A 2-kilometre stretch of dense mahogany trees planted along the national highway in Bilar. The canopy creates a dramatic tunnel effect — one of Bohol's most photographed scenes and a refreshing stop between the Chocolate Hills and Loboc.

Free
Adventure

Hinagdanan Cave

A mystical limestone cave in Dauis, Panglao, featuring dramatic stalactites and stalagmites surrounding a naturally lit underground lagoon. Visitors can swim in the cool, crystal-clear waters inside the cave.

₱75 entrance / ₱150 with swim
Diving

Balicasag Island

A world-renowned marine sanctuary just off Panglao. Divers and snorkelers encounter hawksbill sea turtles, massive coral walls, and abundant reef fish. The island is encircled by a protected marine reserve.

₱250 env. fee + boat fare
Beach

Alona Beach, Panglao

The most famous beach in Bohol — a 1.5-km strip of powdery white sand lined with resorts, restaurants, and dive shops. Perfect as a base for island hopping, diving, and soaking in the Bohol sun.

Free (beach access)
Heritage

Baclayon Church

One of the oldest stone churches in the Philippines, built in 1596 by Jesuit missionaries using coral stones bound with egg whites. Today a National Cultural Treasure, it houses a museum of religious artifacts and antique vestments.

₱50 – ₱150 museum fee
Hidden Gem

Cadapdapan Rice Terraces

Often overlooked, these scenic rice terraces in Candijay offer a serene and uncrowded alternative to northern counterparts. Cascading green paddies flanked by mountains and waterfalls make this one of Bohol's most underrated views.

Free
