Events

Bohol's Festival Calendar

January

Ubi Festival

A celebration of Bohol's prized ube kinampay with food fairs, cooking contests, and local farm showcases.

May

Fiesta Month

Town fiestas bring processions, music, shared meals, and open-house celebrations across many municipalities.

July

Sandugo Festival

The province's headline festival with street dancing, cultural shows, and a reenactment of the historic blood compact.

August

Panagat sa Dauis

A fishing festival centered on coastal life, seafood, and community traditions in Dauis.

Getting There

How to Reach Bohol

Direct flights commonly connect Bohol with Manila, Cebu, and selected domestic hubs depending on the season.
Fast ferries link Cebu City to Tagbilaran and Tubigon, making Bohol an easy side trip from Cebu.
Van with driver: convenient for countryside loops and group day trips.

Self-drive van: best for flexible multi-stop routes.

Scooter: popular around Panglao for short scenic rides.

Tricycle: useful for short local transfers within town centers.

When to Visit

Best Season

Peak Season

December - May

Dry weather, calmer seas, and ideal beach or diving conditions. This is also when the hills take on their chocolate-brown color.

Value Season

June - November

Greener landscapes, fewer crowds, and lower room rates. Outdoor plans still work well between rain showers.

Festival

July - Sandugo Festival

A lively season for cultural events, performances, and city celebrations in Tagbilaran.

Itinerary

Sample 3-Day Countryside + Beach Route

Arrive in Bohol, check in, and spend your first afternoon around Tagbilaran. Visit Baclayon Church and the Blood Compact Shrine, then try local delicacies in the city.
Start with Bilar Manmade Forest and the tarsier sanctuary in Corella. Continue to Loboc River for lunch, then end the day at the Chocolate Hills viewpoint.
Spend the day around Panglao with island-hopping, snorkeling, Hinagdanan Cave, or a slower beach afternoon at Alona.
Add Abatan River fireflies, Candijay's terraces and falls, an Alicia ridgeline hike, or an Anda escape if you want a longer stay.

History

The Sandugo

In 1565, Datu Sikatuna and Miguel Lopez de Legazpi sealed a pact of friendship in what is now Tagbilaran. That ritual, remembered as the Sandugo, remains one of Bohol's strongest historical symbols.


Every July, the Sandugo Festival brings that memory into the present through street dancing, cultural performances, and public celebrations across the city.

Heritage

Francisco Dagohoy

Francisco Dagohoy led the longest rebellion in Philippine history, beginning in 1744 and lasting for 85 years. His resistance became a lasting symbol of Boholano courage and independence.


The story still shapes how Bohol understands itself: resilient, proud, and deeply attached to local identity.

Architecture

Baclayon Church

Founded in 1596, Baclayon Church is one of the oldest stone churches in the country and one of Bohol's most important heritage sites.


Restored after the 2013 earthquake, it remains a strong reminder that Bohol's story is not only natural beauty but also preservation and recovery.
